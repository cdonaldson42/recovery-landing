# Treatment Provider Persona: First-Time Account Creation & Center Claim

## Persona Overview

**Name:** Marcus Chen
**Title:** Executive Director / Owner
**Facility:** New Horizons Wellness Center (18-bed residential)
**Location:** Austin, TX
**Experience:** 12 years as licensed clinical social worker; 2 years as facility owner
**Age:** 44

### Background
Marcus recently opened his own treatment facility after years of working in clinical roles at larger centers. He's clinically strong but newer to the business development side. He's heard about Recovery.com from colleagues at an industry conference and noticed his facility is already listed (from public licensing data) but with incomplete information. He wants to "claim" the listing, correct inaccuracies, and explore what the platform offers. This is his first time creating an account on Recovery.com.

**Technical Comfort:** Moderate. Uses EMR systems daily, comfortable with basic SaaS tools, but not a power user.
**Time Available:** Limited. Wearing multiple hats as owner-operator.
**Budget:** Conservative. Bootstrapped facility, cautious about marketing spend until proven ROI.

**Information He's Willing to Provide:**

## Recommended Portal UX for First-Time Users

### Welcome Dashboard Structure (Post-Enrollment)

**Above the Fold:**
1. **Welcome message** â€” Personalized with facility name
2. **Benefits of Recovery.com** â€” 4 key value props (leads, profile, control, nationwide search)
3. **Next steps banner** â€” "A Provider Ambassador is reviewing your claim"

**Below the Fold:**
4. **Profile completion meter** â€” Visual percentage with checklist
5. **Data on file** â€” Contact info, subscription tier, quick settings access
6. **Build your profile CTA** â€” Photos, reviews, custom content preparation

### Key UX Principles
- **Sell the value immediately** â€” Benefits before features
- **Set clear expectations** â€” Provider Ambassador process with timeline
- **Show progress** â€” Profile completion percentage motivates action
- **Enable preparation** â€” Let them gather content while awaiting verification
- **No dead ends** â€” Even pre-verification, there's something useful to do

### Dashboard Design Principles
- **Progressive disclosure** â€” Show simple view first, advanced features on demand
- **Contextual upsells** â€” "Want more visibility? Learn about Featured Listings" (not modal popups)
- **Mobile-friendly** â€” Marcus might check this on his phone between sessions
- **Clear status indicators** â€” Claim pending, verified, profile % complete

**Notifications That Would Annoy Him:**
