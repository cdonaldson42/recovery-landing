Know your options before you call

Being informed helps you get the right care faster. Here's what to know about paying for treatment.

  • ✔

    Medicaid covers treatment in every state

    Including therapy, medication-assisted treatment, residential care, and outpatient programs

  • ✔

    You can apply for Medicaid at any time

    Many facilities can help you apply on-site, or call your state's Medicaid office

  • ✔

    SAMHSA block grants fund free care

    Federal grants flow to every state specifically for people who can't pay for treatment

  • ✔

    Sliding scale adjusts to your income

    Many community health centers and nonprofits charge based on what you can actually afford

  • ✔

    Treatment scholarships exist

    Organizations like 10,000 Beds, the NAADAC Foundation, and individual facilities offer grants

Free resources you can call right now

All of these are free, confidential, and available 24/7

  • 1
    SAMHSA Helpline: 1-800-662-4357 — treatment referrals in your area
  • 2
    Dial 211 — local assistance for housing, food, healthcare, and treatment
  • 3
    988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline — for crisis support, call or text 988
  • 4
    Crisis Text Line — text HOME to 741741
  • 5
    NAMI Helpline: 1-800-950-6264 — mental health info and support
